# Recommendation Library

40+ ready-to-use recommendations organised by domain and maturity level.
Load this file when generating the Recommendations section of the report.
Pick the most relevant 5–8 based on the client's scores and context.

## Processes & Workflows

### Score 1–2 (Beginner / Aware)
- **Document your top 3 workflows** — Pick the 3 most repeated tasks in your
  business and write them down step by step. This is the single most important
  thing you can do to prepare for AI. Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

- **Identify your biggest time drain** — Track where you or your team spend
  the most manual time over one week. This becomes your first automation target.
  Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Quick win

- **Standardise your inputs** — Before automating anything, make sure the
  data going into a process is consistent (same format, same fields, same source).
  Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Short-term

### Score 3–4 (Ready / Capable)
- **Map trigger-action pairs for your top workflows** — For each documented
  process, define: what starts it, what happens, what the output is. This is
  the blueprint for automation. Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

- **Pilot AI on one workflow** — Choose the most repetitive, well-documented
  process and test one AI tool on it. Measure time saved before scaling.
  Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Short-term

- **Build a process improvement backlog** — Maintain a running list of
  workflow friction points. Prioritise by frequency × time cost.
  Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Quick win

### Score 5 (Advanced)
- **Implement continuous process monitoring** — Use analytics or automation
  logs to track workflow performance over time and catch degradation early.
  Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Short-term

## Data & Quality

### Score 1–2 (Beginner / Aware)
- **Centralise your customer data** — Move customer information into one tool
  (even a simple spreadsheet or free CRM). Scattered data is the #1 blocker
  for AI. Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Short-term

- **Audit your data sources** — List every place business data lives today.
  Identify duplicates, gaps, and the most important data sets to clean first.
  Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Quick win

- **Start collecting structured data** — Replace free-text fields with
  dropdowns, checkboxes, and standardised categories wherever possible.
  Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

### Score 3–4 (Ready / Capable)
- **Implement a basic data hygiene routine** — Monthly: remove duplicates,
  update outdated records, fill in missing fields. Treat data like a business asset.
  Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Quick win

- **Document your data dictionary** — Write down what each data field means,
  where it comes from, and who owns it. Prevents confusion when AI tools
  start using your data. Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Short-term

- **Review GDPR compliance for AI use** — Before feeding customer data into
  AI tools, confirm your privacy policy covers this. A 30-minute legal review
  now prevents problems later. Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

### Score 5 (Advanced)
- **Build a data pipeline for AI inputs** — Automate the flow of clean,
  structured data from your sources into your AI tools. Eliminates manual
  data prep. Effort: High | Impact: High | Strategic

## Tools & Technology

### Score 1–2 (Beginner / Aware)
- **Start with one AI tool, not five** — Pick one high-impact AI tool
  (e.g., Claude, ChatGPT, or a niche tool for your industry) and use it
  daily for 30 days before adding more. Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

- **Audit your current tool stack** — List every tool you pay for and use.
  Identify overlaps, gaps, and tools that could be replaced by AI-native
  alternatives. Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Quick win

- **Enable integrations between your core tools** — Connect your top 2–3
  tools via native integrations or Zapier/Make. This is the foundation for
  automation. Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Short-term

### Score 3–4 (Ready / Capable)
- **Build your first AI-powered automation** — Use Make or n8n to connect
  an AI tool to an existing workflow. Start simple: e.g., auto-summarise
  incoming emails or generate first drafts from form submissions.
  Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Short-term

- **Evaluate AI-native alternatives to legacy tools** — Some of your current
  tools may have AI-powered competitors that do more for the same price.
  Schedule a quarterly tool review. Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Short-term

- **Set up an AI tool policy for your team** — Define which AI tools are
  approved, what data can be shared with them, and how outputs should be
  reviewed. Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

### Score 5 (Advanced)
- **Explore custom AI integrations via API** — Connect AI models directly
  to your business systems for bespoke automation that off-the-shelf tools
  can't provide. Effort: High | Impact: High | Strategic

## Team & AI Maturity

### Score 1–2 (Beginner / Aware)
- **Run an AI awareness session for your team** — A 60-minute workshop
  showing real AI use cases relevant to your business can shift mindset
  faster than anything else. Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

- **Identify your internal AI champion** — Find the one person on the team
  most curious about AI and give them time and permission to experiment.
  This person becomes your change agent. Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

- **Start an AI wins channel** — Create a Slack channel or shared doc where
  team members share AI experiments — good and bad. Normalises experimentation.
  Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Quick win

### Score 3–4 (Ready / Capable)
- **Upskill with structured AI training** — Move beyond ad hoc experimentation
  with a focused training programme on prompt engineering, AI tools relevant
  to your role, and responsible AI use. Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Short-term

- **Build AI into onboarding** — Add AI tool training to your new hire
  onboarding process so every new team member starts AI-capable.
  Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Short-term

### Score 5 (Advanced)
- **Create an internal AI playbook** — Document your team's best AI workflows,
  prompts, and tools in a shared knowledge base. Scales individual expertise
  across the whole team. Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Short-term

## Leadership & Strategy

### Score 1–2 (Beginner / Aware)
- **Define your AI north star** — Write one sentence describing what success
  with AI looks like for your business in 12 months. Everything else flows
  from this. Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

- **Allocate an AI experimentation budget** — Even €500–2,000 for tools,
  training, and consultant time gives you permission to move. Without budget,
  AI stays a conversation. Effort: Low | Impact: High | Quick win

- **Schedule a quarterly AI strategy review** — Block 90 minutes every
  quarter to review what's working, what's changed in the AI landscape,
  and what to prioritise next. Effort: Low | Impact: Medium | Quick win

### Score 3–4 (Ready / Capable)
- **Build an AI roadmap** — Move from reactive AI adoption to a planned
  12-month roadmap with priorities, owners, and success metrics.
  Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Short-term

- **Tie AI initiatives to business KPIs** — Every AI project should map
  to a measurable business outcome (time saved, revenue, cost reduced).
  This justifies investment and keeps focus sharp.
  Effort: Low | Impact: High | Short-term

### Score 5 (Advanced)
- **Position AI as a competitive advantage** — Develop a public narrative
  around your AI capabilities for marketing, recruitment, and partnerships.
  Effort: Medium | Impact: High | Strategic
