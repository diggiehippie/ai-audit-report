# Scoring Rubric — Detailed Criteria & Edge Cases

Use this file when unsure how to score a domain, or when a client's situation
doesn't fit neatly into the main scoring guide.

---

## General Scoring Principles

**Score what you observed, not what the client hopes for.**
If a client says "we're planning to document our processes soon", that's a
future intention — score based on today's reality.

**Weight consistency over peaks.**
A client who does one thing brilliantly but everything else poorly scores
lower than a client who does everything adequately. AI readiness requires
a baseline across all dimensions.

**Consider business size.**
A 2-person business with no documented processes may still score a 3 if
their workflows are simple, consistent, and the founder has full clarity.
A 50-person business with the same situation scores a 1.

**Half-scores are not used.**
Scores are whole numbers 1–5. When in doubt, round down — it's better to
identify a gap than to miss it.

## Domain-Specific Edge Cases

### Processes & Workflows

**Edge case: "Everything is in my head but I'm the only one"**
Solo businesses often run entirely on founder knowledge. If the founder can
clearly articulate their processes verbally and they are consistent, score 2.
If they struggle to describe their own workflows, score 1.

**Edge case: "We use project management tools like Asana/Notion"**
Using a tool doesn't equal documented processes. Check whether the tool
contains actual step-by-step workflows or just task lists. Task lists = 2.
Documented workflows with clear steps = 3 or 4.

**Edge case: Highly variable/creative work**
Some businesses (design agencies, consultants) have inherently variable
workflows. Score on the repeatable parts — client intake, invoicing,
reporting — not the creative core.

---

### Data & Quality

**Edge case: "We have lots of data in our CRM"**
Volume is not quality. Ask: is it clean? Is it complete? Is it used?
A full CRM with outdated records and missing fields scores 2, not 4.

**Edge case: "We don't really collect data"**
Some service businesses genuinely have minimal data needs. Score based on
what they *should* have for their business model, not an abstract standard.
A coach with a simple client list and session notes may legitimately score 3.

**Edge case: GDPR / compliance**
Awareness without action scores 2. Basic compliance (privacy policy, consent
on forms) scores 3. Active data governance with documented processes scores 4–5.

---

### Tools & Technology

**Edge case: "We use lots of tools but nothing is connected"**
Tool quantity is not a positive signal if tools are siloed. A business using
10 unconnected tools scores lower than one using 3 well-integrated ones.
Connectivity and intentionality matter more than volume.

**Edge case: "We tried ChatGPT once"**
Casual experimentation without integration into workflows scores 2, not 3.
Score 3 requires regular, intentional AI tool use that has changed how
at least one task is done.

**Edge case: Technical founder vs. non-technical team**
If the founder is technical but the team isn't, score the team reality —
that's what determines actual AI adoption capacity.

---

### Team & AI Maturity

**Edge case: "My team is resistant but I'm committed"**
Leadership commitment without team buy-in scores 2 for this domain.
Team adoption is the bottleneck — score what the team can actually do today.

**Edge case: Solo business**
For solopreneurs, score the founder's own AI maturity. A solo operator
who uses AI tools daily and understands their capabilities scores 4–5.

**Edge case: "We're hiring AI-savvy people"**
Future hires don't count. Score the current team's capability.

---

### Leadership & Strategy

**Edge case: "The owner wants AI but the board/partners don't"**
Internal misalignment is a risk factor. Score 2 — interest exists but
commitment is not secured.

**Edge case: "We have budget but no clear goal"**
Budget without direction scores 2–3. Strategic clarity matters as much
as financial commitment.

**Edge case: AI interest driven by fear/FOMO vs. genuine problem**
Fear-driven interest ("our competitors are using AI") without a specific
business problem to solve scores lower than problem-driven interest.
FOMO = 2. Clear problem to solve = 3+.

---

## Score Calibration Reference

Use this to sense-check your scores before presenting the report:

| Profile | Expected Score Range |
|---------|---------------------|
| Solo service business, tech-curious, early stage | 2.0–2.8 |
| Small team (2–10), some tools, occasional AI use | 2.5–3.3 |
| Established SME, integrated tools, AI champion present | 3.2–4.0 |
| Tech-forward business, active AI use, clear strategy | 3.8–4.6 |
| AI-native or digitally mature organisation | 4.5–5.0 |

If your score is significantly outside these ranges for a given profile,
revisit your domain answers — something may be over or underweighted.
